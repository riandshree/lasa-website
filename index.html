Who We Are

Built on the belief that education changes everything.

LASA Charitable Foundation is a nonprofit registered under Section 8 of the Companies Act since December 2014. We are dedicated to working with economically poor children, families, and their communities — especially in rural areas.

Our activities are aimed at making education enjoyable and inspiring, and at empowering schools so that even the poorest children get quality education. We also build life skills and empower parents — particularly women — to live productive, independent lives.

Our Purpose

Aim. Mission. Vision.

Our Aim

To ensure all children everywhere are not only able to go to school, but that they get a quality education and learn the skills they need to lead healthy, productive lives and realise their full potential.

Our Mission

We are dedicated to working with economically poor children, families and their communities — especially in rural areas — to ensure that children growing up in poverty get quality education.

Our Vision

To transform lives through education. We believe education will eradicate poverty, boost shared prosperity and broad-based economic growth, and build peaceful, tolerant societies.

Special Initiative

Educate a Girl.

Under the Educate a Girl project, beneficiaries who complete schooling to secondary level are supported with higher education or skill training — helping underprivileged girls break the inescapable cycle of poverty.

Underprivileged girl children are especially protected from child labour, child marriages, and trafficking. Your donation has the power to help these children move closer to their goal.

Special Initiative

Nithya Annadhan Seva.

At Regional Cancer Hospital, Nagpur, LASA runs a daily meal programme for cancer patients' attendants and OPD patients — families already carrying enormous emotional and financial weight.

Because when a family is fighting cancer, the last thing they should worry about is their next meal. A warm plate of food becomes a gesture of dignity, care, and hope.

The Bigger Picture

Why you should care.

Quality education to rural poor is still a distant dream in India. This problem has to be addressed on an urgent basis. Quality education is a high-priced luxury to millions of poor children. The Rural School Quality Education drive of LASA Foundation aids children in reaping the benefits of quality education.

01

13,40,000 children below 5 die in India every year — that is 3,671 under-5 child deaths per day. Nearly half are attributed to under-nutrition.

02

1 in every 11 children in India is working when they should be at school. LASA aims to take every child to school and make education enjoyable and inspiring.

03

17.7 million children and adolescents are out of school in India — 14% of the world's out-of-school population. 53% of children drop out at the elementary level. A large number are girls.

04

20% of grade 2 children cannot recognise numbers 1–9. 49.5% of grade 5 children cannot do basic subtraction. Education quality at the grassroots urgently needs attention.
